Smoke alarms save lives

A working smoke alarm can give you vital early warning of a fire, giving you time to escape. We recommend you:

  • Test your smoke alarms weekly by pressing the test button
  • Replace batteries at least once a year (unless sealed unit)
  • Replace smoke alarms every 10 years
  • Have at least one smoke alarm on every level of your home

Kitchen safety

The kitchen is the most common place for fires to start. Stay safe by:

  • Never leave cooking unattended
  • Keep tea towels and cloths away from the cooker
  • Turn pan handles away from the edge of the hob
  • Never cook when tired or after drinking alcohol

Electrical safety

Faulty electrics cause around half of all accidental fires in the home:

  • Don't overload sockets - one plug per socket is safest
  • Check for warning signs like scorch marks, hot plugs, or flickering lights
  • Replace damaged leads and never use damaged appliances
  • Turn appliances off when not in use, especially overnight

Fire escape plan

Would you know what to do if there was a fire in your home?

  • Plan your escape route and make sure everyone knows it
  • Keep exits clear of clutter
  • Keep door and window keys where everyone can find them
  • If there's a fire, get out, stay out, and call 999
! Warning Never go back into a burning building. Your life is more important than any possession.
