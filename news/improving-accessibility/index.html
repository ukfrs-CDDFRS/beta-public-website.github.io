Digital Transformation

Improving accessibility across our digital services

Author:
Accessibility Lead
Published:
10 December 2025
Reading time:
6 minute read

Accessibility isn't just a legal requirement – it's fundamental to providing public services that work for everyone.

Our accessibility commitment

We're committed to meeting the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 at AA level across all our digital services. This means our services should be:

  • Perceivable - Information must be presentable in ways users can perceive
  • Operable - Interface components must be operable by all users
  • Understandable - Information and operation must be understandable
  • Robust - Content must be robust enough for assistive technologies

Recent improvements

Navigation and structure

We've improved the semantic structure of our pages:

  • Clear heading hierarchy (h1, h2, h3)
  • Skip links to main content
  • Proper landmark regions
  • Keyboard-accessible navigation

Forms and inputs

All forms now include:

  • Associated labels for every input
  • Clear error messages with suggestions
  • Logical tab order
  • Autocomplete attributes where appropriate

Visual design

Our visual updates include:

  • Colour contrast meeting 4.5:1 ratio minimum
  • Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements
  • Text resizable up to 200% without loss of content
  • No content conveyed by colour alone

Testing approach

We test accessibility through multiple methods:

  1. Automated testing - Using axe-core and Lighthouse
  2. Manual testing - Keyboard navigation and screen reader testing
  3. User testing - With people who use assistive technologies
  4. Expert review - Regular audits by accessibility specialists

Reporting issues

If you encounter any accessibility barriers, please:

  • Email us at
  • Use the feedback form on any page
  • Reference the specific page and issue

We aim to respond to accessibility issues within 5 working days.
